From our notebook

The Barcelona House Blog

Stories about our coffee, our food, and our neighbourhood.

Where Our Beans Come From: A Trip to the Nyeri Highlands

Every bag of beans we roast starts on a small farm in the Nyeri highlands, where red volcanic soil and high altitude give Kenyan coffee its signature bright acidity. We visited our growing partners this season to see the harvest firsthand, and came back with a deeper appreciation for the hands behind every cup we serve.

How to Perfect Your Pour-Over at Home

A great pour-over comes down to three things: grind size, water temperature, and patience. We walk through our in-house method step by step, from blooming the grounds to the final slow pour, so you can recreate that Barcelona House cup in your own kitchen.

The Story Behind Our Signature Barcelona Cheesecake

Our cheesecake recipe took eleven attempts before our baker Carlos was satisfied. Inspired by a Catalan-style cheese tart, it's lighter than a New York classic but just as rich, finished with a berry coulis that's become a customer favourite in its own right.

Why We Switched From Oat Milk to Our Matcha Latte

After months of customer requests and our own taste tests, we made oat milk the default in our matcha latte. Its natural sweetness pairs better with ceremonial-grade matcha than dairy does, and it's held up as one of our most popular changes this year.

Barcelona House Turns 2: A Look Back at Our First Open Mic Night

Two years ago we hosted our very first open mic with a handful of nervous performers and a dozen curious customers. Today it's a monthly tradition that fills the café.

The History of Our Building on Woodvale Grove

Before it was Barcelona House, our building spent decades as a family-run print shop. We dug through old records and spoke to long-time neighbours to piece together the story of the space we now call home.
